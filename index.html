Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 1, 2026 · Last Updated: July 5, 2026

1. Introduction

Welcome to OneVictoria, the official Resident Hub of the Municipality of Victoria. Your privacy is fundamental to our mission of transparent and trustworthy governance. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you access our digital services, including account registration, event participation, volunteer applications, and donation transactions.

By using OneVictoria, you consent to the practices described in this policy. We adhere to the Data Privacy Act of 2012 (Republic Act No. 10173) of the Philippines and other relevant local ordinances.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Identification Information: Full name, email address, phone number, age, gender, and residential address provided during account creation or profile updates.
  • Service-Related Data: Volunteer skills, availability, uploaded skill verification documents, service hours records, and event registrations.
  • Donation Records: Donation amounts, purpose, payment method references (GCash, PayMaya, bank transfer), and donor names (if different from account holder).
  • Technical & Usage Data: IP address, browser type, device information, and interaction logs within the Resident Hub for security and analytics.
  • Communications: Messages, feedback, or inquiries you send to municipal staff through the platform.

3. How We Use Your Information

Your data is used strictly for legitimate municipal purposes and to improve community services:

  • To create and manage your resident account, verify identity, and provide secure access to the hub.
  • To process event registrations, volunteer applications, and track community service hours.
  • To facilitate donations, generate receipts, and maintain transparent public donation logs (donor names may be displayed with consent).
  • To send important notifications regarding your activities, announcements, emergency alerts, and policy updates.
  • To improve platform security, debug issues, and analyze aggregated usage trends for better municipal planning.
  • To comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests from government authorities.

4. Data Sharing & Disclosure

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Municipal Departments: Authorized personnel from the Mayor's Office, Social Welfare, Disaster Risk Reduction, and other relevant local government units to coordinate community programs.
  • Service Providers: Trusted third-party vendors (e.g., cloud hosting, payment gateways) who are contractually bound to protect your data and use it solely for designated services.
  • Legal Compliance: When required by law, court order, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of the Municipality of Victoria, its residents, or the public.
  • Public Donor Acknowledgment: With your explicit consent, your name and donation details may appear in public gratitude lists. Otherwise, donations remain anonymous by default.

5. Data Security

We implement robust administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. These measures include encryption in transit (SSL/TLS), secure server infrastructure, role-based access controls, and regular security audits.

Despite our efforts, no method of electronic storage is 100% secure. We continuously monitor and update our practices to align with industry standards and government regulations.

6. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, or as required by Philippine law. Account data is kept while your resident profile is active. Upon account deactivation or request for deletion, we will securely dispose of your data within a reasonable timeframe, unless retention is mandated for legal, auditing, or archival public interest purposes.

7. Your Rights

Under the Data Privacy Act, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal data and request a copy of information we hold.
  • Rectify any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Object to processing or withdraw consent for specific uses (e.g., marketing communications).
  • Request erasure or blocking of your data under certain conditions.
  • File a complaint with the National Privacy Commission (NPC) if you believe your rights have been violated.

To exercise these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer using the details in Section 9. We will respond within the period prescribed by law.

8. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

OneVictoria uses essential cookies and similar technologies to maintain session integrity, remember preferences, and enhance user experience. We do not use third-party advertising cookies. You can control cookie settings through your browser; however, disabling essential cookies may affect platform functionality.

9. Contact Information

For questions, concerns, or to exercise your data privacy rights, please contact:

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. We will notify registered residents via the OneVictoria notification system and post the revised version with a new effective date. Continued use of the platform after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
